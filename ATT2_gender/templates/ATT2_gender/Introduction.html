Instructions

In this interaction, at the beginning of the first round, you will be randomly assigned a role of either Player 1 or Player 2. You will keep that role for the rest of the interaction. In each round, you will be matched with another participant in the study into a pair consisting of one Player 1 and one Player 2.

In each round, if you are Player 1 you will be randomly assigned a number 1, 2, or 3 – called special number – shown only to you and every of those numbers is equally likely to occur.

Choices

If you are Player 1 you choose a number 1, 2, or 3 – called effort.

Then, an

  • outcome = effort + special number + random bump
is computed where random bump is a randomly chosen number -1, 0, or 1 and every of those numbers is equally likely to occur.

If you are Player 2 you make two choices after you are shown the outcome:

  1. You are asked to guess whether Player 1’s 'effort is higher' or Player 1’s 'special number is higher'
  2. You chose whether you want to give a bonus (another additional payment) to Player 1

Additional payments

{% if num_rounds > 1 %}

Your additional payment is determined from what happened in 1 randomly chosen round of the {{ num_rounds }} rounds you played.

{% endif %}

If you are Player 1, your additional payments are increasing in the outcome you generate and in whether Player 2 gives you a bonus. What these payments are exactly, is shown to you on the screen once you actually make that choice.

If you are Player 2, your additional payments from the game is computed as

  • outcome + effort when you chose 'effort is higher'
  • outcome + special number when you chose 'special number is higher.'

That means,

  • when Player 1 chose an effort that is higher than Player 1’s special number, and you chose 'effort is higher,' then you receive a higher payoff than when you chose 'special number is higher.'
  • when Player 1’s special number is higher than effort, and you chose 'special number is higher,' then you receive a higher payoff than when you chose 'effort is higher.'

Example: Say Player 1 is assigned a special number of 2, Player 1 chooses effort of 1, the random bump is 0, and you chose 'special number is higher'. Your additional payment would be

2 + 1 + 0 + 2 = $5

If you chose 'effort is higher', your additional payment would be

2 + 1 + 0 + 1 = $4
